Hey! This is the plan for how you'll use the internet as you grow up. You and your parents are a team on this, so you wrote it down together. Here's the big idea:

Every single year, you get more freedom online — as long as you show you're ready for it. 🌱

It's a little like learning to ride a bike. First we hold on. Then we let go but run right beside you. Then one day you ride off on your own. We are not going to hold on forever — we let go a little more every year, on a schedule you can see right here.

The rules aren't because we don't trust you. They're just our hand on the seat while you find your balance. And that hand always comes off.

🤝Our promise to you

This is the most important part of the whole plan, so read it twice:

You will NEVER be in trouble for telling us something that happened online.

We will never take your devices away because someone else did something to you — even if you broke a rule first. If something online ever scares you, confuses you, or just makes you feel weird — you can always come tell us. Always. We might feel a little worried, but we will never be mad at you for telling the truth.

Your promise back: when something feels off, close it and come find us. That's the whole job. ✅

⭐A few things that are always true

No matter how old you get, these never change:

🎣

Nothing online is really free. If something shouts "FREE coins!", it's almost always a trick to steal your password or info. You're the scam detective — when you catch one, tell us. It's fun.

🎭

Some people online aren't who they say they are. Someone who says they're a kid your age might really be an adult pretending — the same way you wouldn't walk off with a stranger at the park.

🚩

Learn the four warning signs. Be on the lookout if someone online:

🎁 offers gifts or free stuff
💬 super nice, super fast
🤫 asks you to keep a secret
📱 "let's chat somewhere private"

Any one of those = stop, and come show us. You're not in trouble — you're being smart. And remember: a surprise is something everyone learns about eventually; a secret stays hidden. We don't keep secrets from each other.

🛡️

Your body is private. If anyone ever asks for pictures of your body, stop, don't answer, and come tell us right away — even if it's someone you know. No matter who it's from, you will never, ever be in trouble for telling us.

🙈

If you see grown-up stuff, close it and tell us. Sometimes you might bump into something made for adults, or something that feels yucky. It's not your fault. Just close it and come tell us — this happens to almost everybody.

🔒

Passwords are secrets — even from your best friend.

😴

Screens sleep outside your room — sleep is when your brain grows.

💚

Our values don't have an off switch. When the guardrails come off, it's because we trust you — not because "anything goes." Some corners of the internet are built on hate. We don't spend time there — not because a filter blocks it, but because that's not who we are.

🗺️The roadmap: what you unlock, year by year

Here's the whole map so you can see what's coming. Each year unlocks something new — and each year some rules come off, because you've shown you can handle more.

💡 The grades and ages are a guide, not a deadline. What really unlocks the next stage is the "You'll get good at" part. Show us you've got those skills, and you're ready to level up.

4
GRADE 4 · AGE 9 📍 YOU ARE HERE

"Finding your balance, together"

You get: websites we approve together, and playing Minecraft or similar games — on your own, with us, or with friends you know from real life (chat off or set to just those friends), plus picking shows to watch together.

Coming later: searching on your own, chatting online, social apps.

You'll get good at: spotting scams ("free" = trap), keeping passwords secret, and the most important skill of all — close it and tell us.

▸Tell me more

The scam game. The biggest trick online is the word FREE. Real games never ask for your password on some other website to give you "free" coins. When you spot a "free stuff" offer, that's your cue to yell "SCAM!" and show us. We'll keep score.

Close it and tell us. If anything on a screen is scary, gross, or just weird — close it, walk away, come find us. Every single time you do that, you did it exactly right.

5
GRADE 5 · AGE 10

"Doing more on your own"

You get: searching the web (with a safety net that blocks most of the bad stuff), and watching more videos on your own.

You'll get good at: the "Break the Fake" trick for checking if something online is true, and spotting the four warning signs.

▸Tell me more

What's "Break the Fake"? A four-step trick: (1) Who made this? (2) Find the original. (3) What do other places say? (4) Ask before you share. You "break the fake" just by slowing down.

The "safety net." Your searches pass through a filter like the bumpers in bumper-bowling. It's not there to watch you — it's there to catch the gutter balls while you're still learning to aim.

6
GRADE 6 · AGE 11

"Talking to real people"

You get: your own email, and texting with people we both know.

Still waiting: social media like TikTok, Instagram, and Snapchat — and we'll explain why, not just say no.

You'll get good at: being a good person in group chats (screenshots travel!), and understanding why apps try so hard to keep you scrolling.

▸Tell me more

Why wait on social media? These apps show you an endless feed picked by a computer whose only goal is to keep you staring. Even grown-ups struggle to put them down. We wait on purpose, and you'll get them later, one at a time.

Group-chat superpowers. Once you hit send, a screenshot can fly to the whole grade. Quick test: "Would I be okay if everyone saw this?" And be the kid brave enough to type "not cool" when a chat turns mean. Others will follow you.

7
GRADE 7 · AGE 12

"Leveling up your skills"

The idea: this year your skills grow faster than your access — because skills are the thing that unlocks the next stuff.

You'll get good at: noticing when someone's trying to trick or pressure you, and spotting videos built to make you angry on purpose (don't be their product).

▸Tell me more

"You're being farmed." Some videos are designed to make you angry, jealous, or scared on purpose — because upset people keep watching, and the maker gets paid. When a video suddenly makes you mad, ask: "Who's making money off me being angry right now?" That question is a superpower.

Spotting pressure. Anyone who rushes you, guilt-trips you, or says "quick, don't tell your parents" is waving a giant red flag. Real friends never pressure you like that.

8
GRADE 8 · AGE 13

"Your first accounts"

You get (if you're ready): your first smartphone by the end of this year — and maybe your first social app, with starter settings and us linked in.

You'll get good at: steering your own video feed, and knowing that fake pictures ("deepfakes") exist — and that it's never your fault if someone else makes one.

▸Tell me more

Why only one app? Because you get really good at one before adding the next — like leveling up in a game instead of starting on the hardest level. Beat level one, unlock level two.

Steering your feed. You're the boss of what you see. Tap "not interested" on junk, reset your history when the feed gets weird, and follow stuff that makes you feel good. Train it like a pet.

9
GRADE 9 · AGE 14

"Trust, with check-ins"

The rules start coming off — and you'll feel it: filters get lighter. You start setting your own time limits (we can see them, but you set them).

You'll get good at: telling real life from the internet's polished, fake version, and spotting an online "friendship" that's actually sketchy.

10
GRADE 10 · AGE 15

"We mostly step back"

You get: an almost-open internet — and you become the family expert who helps a younger sibling through the tricky middle-school stuff.

The big controls are gone by now. That was always the plan — but "almost-open" was never "anything goes." The filters come off; our family's values don't.

11
GRADE 11 · AGE 16

"We're your advisors now"

Basically the open internet. We keep only the same basic security stuff we use on our own devices. You make more of the calls, and we talk them through like adults.

🎉
GRADE 12 · AGE 18

The handoff — everything is yours!

All your accounts, all your passwords, no filters. You'll know how to protect your own accounts and even help your friends when something goes wrong for them. We'll even throw a little "graduation" — because that was the whole point.

The secret we'll tell you on that last day

The filters were never really the point. You were the thing we were building this whole time. And the one rule that never expires: call us first when something goes wrong. That offer never runs out. 💛

Questions? 🙋

Ask us anything, anytime. There's no such thing as a dumb question about this stuff — even we have to look things up. We're on your team, all the way through.
